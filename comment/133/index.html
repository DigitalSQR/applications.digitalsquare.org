Digital Financial Services on Health Outcomes and Health Systems

Digital Financial Services on Health Outcomes and Health Systems

Submitted by Deepak Batra (IQVIA Solutions (Pty) Ltd) on Wed, 09/25/2019 - 03:00 Last revised by Celia Lang on Mon, 02/03/2020 - 16:36.
Primary Author: Anurag Saxena
Executive summary: 

Digital Square aims to understand whether digital financial solutions can enhance health outcomes and efforts in health systems strengthening in countries where health systems have limited resources. As part of the scope, a two-phased module wise approach will be used for the study. Secondary research will be conducted in Module I to understand the “Impact of Digitization in Digital Financial Services (DFS) on Universal Health Coverage (UHC) and Government schemes for Disease and Family Planning in three geographies (India, Egypt and UAE)”.

We will be collecting the data from different Ministries/ Health Departments, various stakeholders, financial service provider and institutions that are actively involved in health care financing. We will also be taking consideration of various case studies implemented in different countries which impact Health System Strengthening. The objective of the study will be to analyze:

1.     Reduction in Government Expenditure

2.     Reduction in out-of-pocket expense of beneficiaries

3.     Reach Out – Increase in number of beneficiaries

4.     Improved Performance of Health Systems

In Module-II, primary research will be conducted in selected geographies in India, Egypt and UAE within a span of 6 months to gauge the impact of digitization on financial health protection. The primary search will measure the impact of digitization in Digital Financial Services for UHC and TB Schemes in these countries.

IQVIA has successfully executed more than 150 large scale, health care monitoring, evaluation, research studies and surveys on improving quality of care, performance of healthcare systems, utilization of healthcare services and digital health service provision. We will leverage our domain expertise and existing client base in the areas of UHC, Government schemes associated with Disease Management and Family Planning.

Technical feedback

Celia Lang - Thu, 12/05/2019 - 15:38

We advise that the proposed research inquiry as written does not resonate with the intended purpose of the RFA, which is to landscape the impact of DFS on financial protection (in an effort to advance progress towards UHC) and understand the effect of DFS and digitization writ large on both the supply/production of health and the demand/utilization of health services. Given the intent of the RFA is to focus on understanding the effect of digital solutions on health systems performance, a research agenda which focuses on a health element, e.g. family planning reduces the scope of the intended research.
